01 — Problem & Opportunity
The problem worth solving
Saffa · June 2026
Project overview
Saffa is a lightweight inventory and waste-tracking tool for independent café owners in Riyadh who currently manage stock through WhatsApp groups, handwritten lists, or memory. The product runs on mobile and requires no training.
The problem
Independent café owners in Saudi Arabia lose an estimated 12–18% of their monthly revenue to over-ordering, spoilage, and untracked wastage. The problem is invisible until end-of-month P&L reveals it — by then, the loss has already happened. There is no affordable, Arabic-language tool that addresses this for single-location operators.
Who experiences it
First or second-generation Saudi entrepreneurs running one to three café locations in Riyadh, Jeddah, and Dammam. Typically aged 24–38, digitally comfortable, but without formal operations training. Annual revenue between SAR 800,000 and SAR 2,500,000.
Why current solutions fail
Enterprise solutions like Lightspeed and Toast are priced for chains, require training, and are English-only. Local POS vendors bolt on inventory as an afterthought. Nothing is designed for the daily operating rhythm of a single-location independent operator.
Problem scale

"I check my numbers at the end of the month and there's always a gap I can't explain. I know it's the kitchen but I can't see it in real time." — Café owner, Al-Olaya, Riyadh

08 — Risks & Mitigation
What could go wrong
Saffa · June 2026
Risk assessment
RiskLikelihoodMitigation plan
Café owners don't log daily — the product loses value if habit doesn't formHighDaily 11pm push notification with single-tap quick log. Onboarding includes a 7-day guided streak. Pilot data shows 78% daily active use after week 2.
POS vendors copy the feature — incumbents add inventory as a free add-onMediumSpeed and focus are the moat. Building switching costs through daily habit and 90-day historical data is the long-term defence.
Price sensitivity — SAR 149/month is too high for some operatorsMediumROI framing from day one: "Saffa costs SAR 149. The average café saves SAR 1,800/month in waste within 30 days." Freemium tier for smallest operators as a conversion funnel.
Regulatory change — ZATCA or SFDA introduce new F&B data requirementsLowSaffa's data model is structured for compliance by default. Any new requirement becomes a feature, not a blocker.
What could kill this in year 1
If café owners do not form a daily logging habit, the product becomes shelfware and churns within 90 days. This is the single existential risk. Every product and retention decision for the first 12 months is designed around this one threat.
